# Quality Drives Growth

## Purpose
This skill helps identify when the best growth move is to improve quality in a critical flow instead of layering on experiments or incremental optimizations.

## Use this skill when
- A funnel or onboarding flow has clear usability pain
- A team is over-indexing on small tests while the experience is visibly rough
- Conversion is lagging in a high-intent journey
- There is a need to decide between quality work and more experimentation

## Do not use this skill when
- The journey is already high quality and the problem is traffic or market fit
- The issue is only instrumentation and not experience quality
- The team has not identified any meaningful UX breakdowns

## Core principles
1. Experience quality can be a bigger growth lever than clever optimization.
2. High-intent flows deserve high-quality execution.
3. Broken continuity, confusion, and cognitive overload kill conversion.
4. Growth teams should sometimes stop optimizing and fix the product.
5. Quality work should still be tied to measurable outcomes.

## Workflow
1. Identify the target journey.
2. Audit for quality failures:
   - unclear language
   - broken progression
   - cognitive overload
   - too many fields at once
   - poor error handling
   - trust/confidence issues
3. Rank the quality issues by likely impact.
4. Recommend a quality-first remediation plan.
5. Define what experimentation should pause or continue during the effort.
6. Define the before/after metrics to track.

## Output format
Return:
- journey assessed
- major quality issues
- why they matter
- prioritized fixes
- what to pause
- expected conversion or activation impact
- measurement plan

## Anti-patterns
- Treating obvious UX debt as an experimentation problem
- Optimizing copy around a broken flow
- Focusing only on click-through and ignoring user confusion
- Assuming quality work is “not growth work”
